DC POWER SUPPLY

CONCEPT

The DC Power Supply is a variable supply built from a regular scrap PC ATX Power Supply Unit. It has an output port for supplying VGA devices, and a variable output which reaches 1.2V to 10.2V.



The voltage regulation is done via three LM 317T voltage regulator packages, two of which are set permanetly to 3.3V and 5V and used for VGA power, and one with a potentiometer for varying output.

The voltage is then displayed via seven segment displays which are controlled through a PIC microcontroller. Finally, in order for the ATX supply to function, there must be a constant load recognized by the internal circuitry.
